It took me a while to get to the main idea that I wanted to develop for this basic game text but in the end I managed to developing the concept thanks to specific references, like Dungeons and Dragons, older RPG classic games, such as The Elder Scrolls III Morrowind or even simple decision-making games, like Fallout New Vegas. Particularly, when placing the corresponding inputs and how much separation space was necessary in between the different segments of the code. I tried not to be dependent on the AI as I wanted to develop my own personal programming and designing skills, since I have always been interested in making my own games. The very first concept of Dungeon Exploration was kind of shallow. It was not intended to have three specific routes. In the very first begining, I thought of only making two main routes based on melee combat and stealthy. I even thought about adding extra elements, such as magic, potions, health restoration and so on but managed to discard those elements since made no actual sense for the simple text game. Also, I had no clear idea how to implement these elements in order to make the game function properly. The idea is the following:
1) Left route: is basically based on the use of strenght. In which you have to defeat an enemy. I wanted to introduce a goblin, which is the basic enemy in most of the RPG games or even MMORPGs (such as RuneScape, which is also a huge inspiration for this game). But afterwards I decided to introduce an orc to make the experience much more challenging. If you fail in this part, you literally just die and could not get the treasure. Therefore, the entire quest/adventure has to be considered as a huge failure
2) Right route: is mostly inspired on The Hobbit. The dragon sleeping is basically a reference to Smaug in the Tolkienverse. The main part of this entire area is to sneak through in order to get the treasure. If you make noise, you literaly die. The dragon will awaken and your character will eventually colapse to the neverending fire of it. I did not add this part as a print but it is suggested in the way the game tells you that you have failed. 
For the center route, ffter a long while fixing and adding narrative aspects in order to make the player's experience much more engaging and interactive, I considered the idea of introducing the center route as part of the game. In the beginning I had the clear intention of introduction a sphynx, which is a clear reference to the puzzles that Harry Potter had to solve during the Goblet of Fire book in the last part of the Tournament Trials. But this idea had problems in terms of being feasible. How is it possible to place a sphynx in the middle of a dungeon? For the two riddles, I had to look them up on Google as I did not want to make the game excesively confusing. I had a lot of fun though but since this is my actual first practice of creating a simple game (not counting the mini inividual coding project which was basically made by AI), I did not want to make it extremely complex. 
After a long struggle with fixing the codes, I had to change the entire prints since those were in input mode and could not be showed as part of the narrative. ######## H8
For this assignment I tried to do something more experimental. More connected also to my storytelling interests but also trying to implement the requirements for the assignment in terms of assigning the corresponding functions, libraries and inventory management as required. I will now explain the entire process that it lead me in terms of developing the entire concept for "Memories: A psychological horror experience", addressing the resources that I used in order to understand not only the result but also the reflective and creative process behind it. 

Resources
For this game I used the template that was provided in PyCharm in order to create the game and I had to use Claude AI in order to understand the structure of it, how to implement it and especially to give me feeback in terms of which areas where not working and so on. Nevertheless, I wrote the entire code on my own following those instructions and trying to understand how to provide not only the descriptive elements of each of the objects that the player needs to use throughout this interactive experience but also how to define each of the aspects, such as the functions for using, examining, the loop itself and the ending which also was problematic as I had to change a lot of elements in order to make it function. Also, as an extra resources I took inspiration on classical survival horror games, such as Silent Hill 2 and Resident Evil which I will later on further expalin in the next section of this assignment explanation. It took me a long while to understand what I was actually doing as I had to develop the idea in different ways, had to test it out a couple of times in order to check if there were some bugs or errors that provided problems for the players.

Game and story concept
In the very first beginning, after the explanation of the assignment I had a clear idea that I wanted to do a survival horror game as it perfectly fit within the inventory managamenet. As a great fan of classical Resident Evil games, this served as a perfect excuse to create a hostile environment in which the player had to make decisions in order to survive, either using health items, managing resources, keys, weapons, ammunition and so on. The first idea I had was basically trying to emulate the Hospital section of Silent Hill 2 in which the player has to navigate a couple of narrow corridors and face enemies (which I had all of them defined in a specific section called Enemies, with its corresponding life points, damage and a description of each creature). It was kind of a mixture between the psychological horror elements of SH2 and the body horror elements that are heavily present in the RE franchise. Nevertheless, I had a lot of problems while coding it as adding combat features, action and defining the mechanics was impossible and I felt extremely overwhelmed by this first idea. I had to change and rewrite everything again and resulted in a lot of overwheling frustration trying to achieve thi goal. 
After a long time of reflection and writing on my notebook the main concepts that I wanted to address, the whole idea of the hospital evolved. I had couple of other alternative concepts, such as a small escape room. After getting some feedback by other friends, as well, and reflecting on my own I came to the conclusion that I needed to make the game simpler. Of course, it kinda makes sense when you are starting to learn a new skill. But sometimes you have to go through this process to fully understand the basics. Kinda ironic, though. 

Afterwards, I started writing the concepts again and the idea evolved to a character that we do not its name. It used to be a person but then it became something off. Something strange. We do not know much of this character. And that's the point. I liked this idea and then I kept some elements such as the hospital and then decided to implement concepts such as social isolation, experimentation, deprivation from all sorts of emotions and in the end this idea evolved to the game that I added on this GitHub. The following story is basically a person that wakes up in the middle of a strange, claustrophobic and boring room. You have forgotten everything. All your past memories, who you used to be, what you were, your goals, future, dreams, relationships, etc. are no longer important. You need to reconstruct what happened. There's also a lot of missing past information that I decided to leave it as a mystery for the player to interpret. 

Something is off from the very first beginning. The room, the way that the character is being under hipervigillance. By who? Or by what? Is it real? Or mere fiction? Is it a product from his own imagination? I decided to keep this idea kinda ambiguous. The player needs to interact with a couple of objects in an specific order for the narrative to make sense. It is intended to be played from the order the game provides you since the very first beginning as throughout the progressive interaction with each of the objects will give you pieces of the story. A note that contains a love confession; a newspaper that provides a tragic event that will give you hints; a journal that gives you more background information; a photography that also gives the player more clues in relation to the identity of this mysterious character and then we reach the end: the keycard. All these interactions with each object are also followed by a mysterious voice that is always punishing and making the character realize what happened. I took this idea from Silent Hill 2 and serves also the purpose of making the player reflect on what happened, as well as to provide a sense of dread and fear within the game

When the player acquires the keycard the story is revealed along with the emotional reaction of the main character. The uses and examine functions of the game are strictly related to the narrative and after you obtain the keycard you receive another item. The player has to decide to drop one item. It does not affect the ending but it's symbolic that in order to escape the player has to drop a part of his identity/memory. After this, the player truly escapes? I decided to keep the ending ambigous. 

However, I know there are maybe major problems with the narrative and that there are also a lot of aspects to improve, in order to make it more coherent but it is a small experiment. I decided not only to create a small inventory game but also added a lot of narrative elements, using aspects that we have learned before. Using time.sleep for the pacing and prints for the player to see dialogues and the reaction of the character and this shady companion, that is The Voice, to make it more engaging. It was not mandatory for the assignment but I tried to combine this practice of creating libraries and dictionaries with storytelling elements. It took me a lot of work to create the final results. I had to rewrite the code over an over, ask Claude for further feedback and areas to improve. Asked questions in terms of understanding all the possible aspects that are present in the code. 

Even though it is not perfect and needs a lot of refinement, I am very happy with its result and also inspires me for future storytelling projects within the Game sector :)
